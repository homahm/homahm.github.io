About Me

I am a Postdoctoral fellow working with Professor Emilio Ferara at the Information Science Institue at University of Southern California.

My research interests lie in computational social science, applied machine learning, statistical inference, time series modeling and data minig.

My Research


My research interests lie at the interface of computational social science, statistical inference and applied machine learning, wherein I am interested in questions around making sense of human behavior. I am motivated by how advances in sensing technology and the availability of large-scale online social data, coupled with computational methods can help us answer fundamental questions varying from wellbeing and performance to misbehavior. My research agenda thus makes extensive use of applied machine learning, applied statistics, social and behavioral science, and human-centered approaches.

I love developing machine learning methods for analyzing the structure, content, and dynamics of social processes. My work is inherently interdisciplinary and I collaborates with psychologist, sociologists, and bio-engineers to understand how predictive human behavior is from biological signals such as heart activity or from social activity such as online media usage. To complement this agenda, I also studies issues of geralizeability and fairness in longitudinal and observational studies, which are important concerns when working on decision making and human subjects.
